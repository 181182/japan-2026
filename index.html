🎫 JR pass strategy

Your big legs are spread across 18 days, so an all-you-can-ride nationwide pass is poor value. Mix individual tickets with one regional pass instead.

Bottom line: Skip the ~¥100,000 nationwide pass. Individual tickets for the north + a Kansai-Hiroshima Area Pass for the west ≈ ¥62,000/person. All figures approximate 2026 — verify at booking.

❌ Nationwide JR Pass avoid

21-day pass ≈ ¥100,000. Even buying every ticket individually (~¥64,700) beats it by ~¥35,000. Built for people criss-crossing the country in one week — not your pace.

The Hokuriku Arch Pass (¥35,000 since Mar 2026) also loses to individual tickets here because you travel that route one-way, not round-trip.

The one catch ⚠️

The Kansai-Hiroshima Pass is 5 consecutive days, so it can't cover both Hiroshima (Jul 14) and the airport run (Jul 20) — 6 days apart. Activate it Jul 14 (it then covers Hiroshima + Miyajima ferry + Nara + Osaka→Kyoto + Kyoto local trains through Jul 18), and buy the final Kyoto→KIX Haruka separately.

💳 IC cards & local transport

One tap-card each for everything off-pass; add a city pass only where it clearly pays.

IC card — get this first

Easiest: add Suica or ICOCA to your phone wallet (especially iPhone) — no queue, no deposit, top up with your card. Physical: grab an ICOCA at KIX on arrival. Works on metros, buses, local trains, konbini, and vending machines nationwide. Start with ¥3,000–5,000 loaded.

🟣 Kanazawa

Buy the Loop Bus day pass (~¥600) — hits every sight, saves your legs. From the East Gate bus terminal.

🟢 Nagano

Just tap your IC card. Bus line 1 to Zenkoji; trains to Matsumoto. No pass needed.

🩷 Tokyo

Tokyo Subway Ticket (24h ¥800 / 48h ¥1,200 / 72h ¥1,500) for metro days. IC card for JR Yamanote hops.

🟠 Osaka

Mostly walkable from your hotel. IC card for the subway. Osaka Amazing Pass (~¥3,300) only if you cram a big sightseeing day.

🔵 Kyoto

The ¥700 bus day pass was scrapped in 2024. Tap your IC card on buses, or get the subway+bus pass (~¥1,100). Taxi between far temples — cheap and easy on the legs.

🧳 Baggage forwarding

Takuhaibin (Yamato "Kuroneko"): ~¥2,000–2,500 per suitcase per hop, next-day hotel-to-hotel. The biggest comfort upgrade of the trip.

🥃 Breweries & distilleries

Full-scale, proper operations with real guided tours — no basement nano-breweries.

Suntory Yamazaki Distillery book ASAP · lottery

Birthplace of Japanese whisky, maker of Yamazaki single malt. Real mash tuns, washbacks, pot stills and ageing warehouses. On your Osaka–Kyoto line — 10 min walk from JR Yamazaki Station (covered by your pass). Craftsmanship Tour: 80 min, ¥3,000, exclusive tastings. Museum + tasting lounge as the backup if tours sell out. English available, 20+ only.

Best slot: a half-day from Kyoto (Jul 18/19), or a stop on the Jul 17 move. Tours run on a monthly lottery — enter the instant July opens.
→ Book → Maps

Asahi Suita Factory Osaka

Birthplace of Asahi, recently rebuilt with projection mapping, a VR "dive into the fermentation tank," and a Super Dry ride — then fresh Super Dry tastings. ~¥1,000, ~1h. 10 min from Suita Station (on your pass). Reserve online.

→ Book → Maps

Gekkeikan + Fushimi Kyoto · Jul 18

World's leading sake producer; Meiji-era working brewery in Fushimi, Kyoto's sake quarter (Gekkeikan, Kizakura & more). ~¥600 with tastings + a bottle gift. Same area as Fushimi Inari — shrine at sunrise, sake by late morning.

→ Maps

Fukumitsuya Kanazawa · Jul 3

Revered sake house since 1625, brewery + tasting salon open to visitors, 5 min from the samurai district. Gold-leaf sake is the local signature. Fits your Jul 3 day.

→ Maps

Saijo Sake Town Jul 14 add-on

One of Japan's three great sake regions — 8+ breweries on Sakagura-dori, most open for tasting, by Saijo Station (~30 min from Hiroshima, on your pass). Peace memorial morning + sake afternoon.

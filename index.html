🌡️ Essentials & safety

July is peak summer: 30–35 °C, very humid, and the tail of the rainy season into mid-month. Heat is the single biggest risk on this trip — plan indoor escapes for 1–4pm and drink constantly.

☀️ Heat strategy: aim outdoor sights for before 11am or after 5pm. Carry a water bottle, a cooling towel, and a hand fan; refill at any konbini. Convenience stores, department basements (depachika), museums, and aquariums are your air-conditioned reset points. Watch for heatstroke — dizziness, headache, stopped sweating means stop, cool down, and rehydrate with salt (pocari/aquarius).

🏥 Useful to know

Embassy (Tokyo): register your trip with your home country's embassy and save its address + phone before you fly.

Pharmacies: look for ドラッグストア (drugstore) — Matsumoto Kiyoshi, Sundrug. Bring any prescription meds in original packaging with a copy of the prescription.

Lost item? Japan's lost-and-found is excellent — report at the nearest station office or kōban (police box).

💴 Money & budget

Japan is still surprisingly cash-friendly — many small restaurants, shrines, and lockers are cash-only. Carry ¥10,000–20,000 (≈700–1,300 kr) on you and top up as you go.

💱 NOK conversions shown throughout use ≈¥15 = 1 kr (mid-2025 ballpark — the yen has been weak, great for you). Check the live rate: JPY → NOK.

💳 Cash & cards

Withdraw yen at 7-Eleven ATMs (in every konbini) or Japan Post ATMs — both reliably accept foreign Visa/Mastercard. Notify your bank you'll be in Japan to avoid blocks.

IC cards (Suica/ICOCA) cover trains, buses, konbini and vending — top up with cash at any station machine. Cards/Apple Pay work in chains, but always keep cash for the small places.

🛍️ Tax-free shopping

Spend ¥5,000+ (≈330 kr) in one store in one day and claim ~10% tax-free at the counter — bring your passport. Great for the electronics (Yodobashi) and fabric/craft hauls. Goods are sealed; don't open until you leave Japan. Keep receipts for the customs check on departure.

🎫 JR pass strategy

Your big legs are spread across 18 days, so an all-you-can-ride nationwide pass is poor value. Mix individual tickets with one regional pass instead.

Bottom line: Skip the ~¥100,000 (≈6,700 kr) nationwide pass. Individual tickets for the north + a Kansai-Hiroshima Area Pass for the west ≈ ¥62,000/person (≈4,100 kr). All figures approximate 2026 — verify at booking.

❌ Nationwide JR Pass avoid

21-day pass ≈ ¥100,000 (≈6,700 kr). Even buying every ticket individually (~¥64,700, ≈4,300 kr) beats it by ~¥35,000 (≈2,330 kr). Built for people criss-crossing the country in one week — not your pace.

The Hokuriku Arch Pass (¥35,000 (≈2,330 kr) since Mar 2026) also loses to individual tickets here because you travel that route one-way, not round-trip.

The one catch ⚠️

The Kansai-Hiroshima Pass is 5 consecutive days, so it can't cover both Hiroshima (Jul 14) and the airport run (Jul 20) — 6 days apart. Activate it Jul 14 (it then covers Hiroshima + Miyajima ferry + Nara + Osaka→Kyoto + Kyoto local trains through Jul 18), and buy the final Kyoto→KIX Haruka separately.

💳 IC cards & local transport

One tap-card each for everything off-pass; add a city pass only where it clearly pays.

IC card — get this first

Easiest: add Suica or ICOCA to your phone wallet (especially iPhone) — no queue, no deposit, top up with your card. Physical: grab an ICOCA at KIX on arrival. Works on metros, buses, local trains, konbini, and vending machines nationwide. Start with ¥3,000–5,000 (≈200–330 kr) loaded.

🟣 Kanazawa

Buy the Loop Bus day pass (~¥600, ≈40 kr) — hits every sight, saves your legs. From the East Gate bus terminal.

🟢 Nagano

Just tap your IC card. Bus line 1 to Zenkoji; trains to Matsumoto. No pass needed.

🩷 Tokyo

Tokyo Subway Ticket (24h ¥800 / 48h ¥1,200 / 72h ¥1,500 · ≈55/80/100 kr) for metro days. IC card for JR Yamanote hops.

🟠 Osaka

Mostly walkable from your hotel. IC card for the subway. Osaka Amazing Pass (~¥3,300, ≈220 kr) only if you cram a big sightseeing day.

🔵 Kyoto

The ¥700 bus day pass was scrapped in 2024. Tap your IC card on buses, or get the subway+bus pass (~¥1,100, ≈75 kr). Taxi between far temples — cheap and easy on the legs.

🥃 Breweries & distilleries

Full-scale, proper operations with real guided tours — no basement nano-breweries.

Suntory Yamazaki Distillery book ASAP · lottery

Birthplace of Japanese whisky, maker of Yamazaki single malt. Real mash tuns, washbacks, pot stills and ageing warehouses. On your Osaka–Kyoto line — 10 min walk from JR Yamazaki Station (covered by your pass). Craftsmanship Tour: 80 min, ¥3,000 (≈200 kr), exclusive tastings. Museum + tasting lounge as the backup if tours sell out. English available, 20+ only.

Best slot: a half-day from Kyoto (Jul 18/19), or a stop on the Jul 17 move. Tours run on a monthly lottery — enter the instant July opens.

Asahi Suita Factory Osaka

Birthplace of Asahi, recently rebuilt with projection mapping, a VR "dive into the fermentation tank," and a Super Dry ride — then fresh Super Dry tastings. ~¥1,000 (≈67 kr), ~1h. 10 min from Suita Station (on your pass). Reserve online.

Gekkeikan + Fushimi Kyoto · Jul 18

World's leading sake producer; Meiji-era working brewery in Fushimi, Kyoto's sake quarter (Gekkeikan, Kizakura & more). ~¥600 (≈40 kr) with tastings + a bottle gift. Same area as Fushimi Inari — shrine at sunrise, sake by late morning.

Fukumitsuya Kanazawa · Jul 3

Revered sake house since 1625, brewery + tasting salon open to visitors, 5 min from the samurai district. Gold-leaf sake is the local signature. Fits your Jul 3 day.

Saijo Sake Town Jul 14 add-on

One of Japan's three great sake regions — 8+ breweries on Sakagura-dori, most open for tasting, by Saijo Station (~30 min from Hiroshima, on your pass). Peace memorial morning + sake afternoon.

Mars Shinshu Distillery Nagano · whisky

Hombo Shuzo's highland malt distillery in the Central Alps — one of Japan's highest, makers of the award-winning Iwai & Mars whiskies. Free self-guided tour of the stills + a paid tasting flight and bottle shop. ~1h from Nagano toward Komagane; a great half-day detour for whisky fans.

The Kyoto Distillery — Ki No Bi gin Kyoto · gin

Japan's first dedicated craft gin distillery, using yuzu, hinoki, gyokuro tea & sansho pepper. Production tours are limited/by appointment, but you can taste the full Ki No Bi range at top Kyoto bars — ask for the flight.

Nada Sake District — Hakutsuru Kobe · sake

Japan's largest sake region, on your Kansai pass via Kobe. The Hakutsuru Sake Brewery Museum (free) shows traditional brewing with English signage + tastings; Sawanotsuru & Kiku-Masamune are next door. Easy add-on between Osaka and Hiroshima.

🍸 Cocktail & whisky bars

Japan makes some of the world's best cocktails — precise, quiet, ice carved by hand. Below are standout bars plus a ready-made bar-to-bar crawl for each city. Many seat <10 people; arrive early or book. Expect a small seating/snack charge (otōshi).

Etiquette: at high-end bars keep it low-key — no big groups shouting. A cover charge of ¥500–1,500 (≈35–100 kr) is normal. Cash is safest. "Omakase" lets the bartender build to your taste.
